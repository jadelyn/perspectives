2014 ASUC Perspectives Festival
The Space Between Our Footsteps

About Perspectives


Perspectives, UC Berkeley's Multicultural Showcase, focuses on the space between our footsteps with the hxstories (an “x” is used to validate every story), and experiences every student carries with them as they move through campus. How do we connect our past experiences to our future struggles and achievements? How do we come to terms with our perspectives of one another based on trials and tribulations?

The discomfort, angst, and words never spoken often fall into the spaces between our footsteps as we travel along our own paths. Perspectives serves as a space in which students and the community at large can intentionally engage with themes such as race, class, gender, sexuality, religion, and hxstories through all volumes of art.

This year, ASUC Perspectives takes on the form of a 3 night festival, with a different set of performances each night. Join us in celebrating the talents and diversity of UC Berkeley.

Performance Lineup

April 18th

Danceworx

Danceworx was founded in 1988 and is the oldest student-run dance group on the UC Berkeley campus. For over 20 years, we have been providing students with a creative outlet to choreograph, dance, and perform in a fun and supportive environment. Dancers are selected at the beginning of each semester and practice routines in various styles: Jazz, Lyrical, Hip hop, and Tap.

Fei Tian Dancers

Fei Tian Dancers is the Chinese dance group on campus. We perform dances from various geographical locations and time periods across China, and hope to help spread Chinese culture to the greater community.

Magical Nathaniel

Magical Nathaniel has been performing magic for 16 years and has done hundreds of shows. He has won many magic competitions and is a member and past historian for the Oakland Magic Circle. He currently teaches the Magic DeCal taught on campus. Magical Nathaniel also designs and builds many of the illusions that he will be performing tonight.

Mariachi Luz de Oro

Mariachi Luz de Oro de Berkeley is a student-run musical group that performs traditional and modern mariachi music. We aspire to promote cultural diversity by increasing exposure to the Mexican art form.Our ensemble has participated in events in and around the Berkeley area, including an encore performance alongside the world-renowned Mariachi de Tecalitlán. Our members hope to continue to bring music and joy to the Cal community.

Berkeley Ballroom

For over 50 years, the student-run Berkeley Ballroom has provided ballroom dancers with classes, social dance events, and ballroom competitions on the UC Berkeley campus. Our events encompass all levels of dancers, from those just beginning to learn the steps to social dancing all the way up to top DanceSport competitors competing in collegiate and professional competitions across the nation. Ultimately our goal is to help the next generation strengthen their personalities and gain exposure to new experiences via the creative outlet of ballroom dancing. Check out our Facebook page, Cal Ballroom, to learn more about our team.

April 19th

Universal Love and Peace (ULAP)

ULAP is an organization that intends to spread awareness on human rights. In unison with supporting groups, ULAP achieves this goal through community service, musical performances, demonstrations, fundraising events, community service retention and outreach, peaceful dialogue, speeches, weekly tutoring, and human rights proposals for legislative change. The ULAP band has helped fundraise over $39,000 and performs for many organizations to raise awareness of social injustices through original songs and covers about various human rights topics. The band has produced a CD that sold-out in less than two weeks, which invokes the voices of our communities through music.

Sorayya Bellydance

Sorayya is a dance troupe affiliated with UC Berkeley, dedicated to the collectivization and expression of dance techniques found throughout the Middle East, Central Asia and North Africa. We respect the purity of dance as human expression, while utilizing its ability to evolve into our own personalized story.

Kol Hadov

Kol Hadov is UC Berkeley's one and only Jewish a cappella group. We sing about half Jewish/Hebrew music and half secular music while creating lasting friendships with one another. Kol Hadov performs on the Berkeley campus, at Hillel, and at other community events in the Bay Area.

Celli@Berkeley

Celli@Berkeley was created one night in 2012 after the cellists of the UC Berkeley Symphony Orchestra realized that the only thing better than one cello was many, many cellos. Although consisting of twelve undergraduate and graduate students studying a variety of academic disciplines (including statistics, environmental science, history, chemistry, biology, and public health), our group is united by a desire to express the possibilities of the cello. Celli@Berkeley embodies our belief that anyone can be a musician and both enjoy and learn something from live music, no matter what style it is or in what setting - a concert hall, an art museum, or even on the streets of Berkeley!

Iranian Students Cultural Organization

Iranian Students' Cultural Organization (ISCO), established in 1988, is one of the largest minority organizations at UC Berkeley. We promote the interest and awareness of Iranian culture in Berkeley community. Each year, ISCO hosts several events in which members engage in fun and interesting activities while meeting new people. Some of our well-known events are listed below. So come and join us for a fun and exciting year.

Rare Candy

Rare Candy, a UC Berkeley duo, is composed of Jenny Chen and Somin Lim. For more info: check out our Facebook Page at www.facebook.com/officialrarecandy.

April 20th

Irish Dancers of Berkeley

The Irish Dancers of Berkeley, established in Fall 2013, aims to provide a time, place, and platform for Irish dancers in the Berkeley community to practice, perform, and explore all aspects of this sport and art form, while bringing a bit of craic to Cal! We do regular stamina and technique workouts, but also choreograph traditional and contemporary performance routines. We actively seek opportunities to perform in and around Berkeley, and we hope to eventually compete in intercollegiate competitions and host a DeCal! This art form is thriving in the Bay Area, and Cal should be connected to the Irish community — the Irish Dancers of Berkeley are proud to serve as cultural ambassadors around campus, the Bay, and beyond.

Korean Performance Group

Founded in 2010, KPG Cal currently the only permanent dance group that represents K-Pop culture in UC Berkeley. Through performances and dancing, KPG strives to deliver the arts of K-Pop culture to the diverse UC Berkeley community so that more people can learn the Korean Culture. KPG Cal's stories revolve around our passion for Korean music and performance as 22 dancers strive to represent an international culture while bringing an unique form of art to the diverse UC Berkeley campus.

Dil Se

Founded in 2004, Dil Se is Berkeley's only co-ed Sounth Asian a cappella group, combining the melody-based ragas of Indian music with the Western a cappella tradition. The Group brings together a wide rained of experience, from years of training in Hindustani and Carnatic music, to Western Classical and Jazz Choirs, and even countless mornings of singing in the shower. Our goal is to entertain the audience with a fresh take on music that emanates Dil Se, or "from the heart."

Natya at Berkeley

Natya at Berkeley is the premier Bharatanatyam team at UC Berkeley. Founded in Spring 2013, Natya at Berkeley aims to cultivate artistic talent and cultural awareness within the community.

Cal Kathak

Kathak is one of eight different classical dance forms of India, and originated in the temples of northern India. Kathak, meaning "story," is a dance form that uses technical footwork, spins, and expressions to tell various stories. Cal Kathak is a group that dances in various on-campus, multicultural performances.
